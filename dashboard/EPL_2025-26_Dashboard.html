Data source

Match-by-match results and statistics for the 2025–26 Premier League season, sourced from football-data.co.uk via its DataHub mirror (season-2526.csv). 22 columns per match: scoreline, half-time scoreline, referee, shots, shots on target, fouls, corners, and cards for each side.

Data cleaning

  • Verified completeness: 380 rows (20 teams × 38 matches), each team appearing exactly 38 times, zero duplicate fixtures.
  • Checked for missing values across all 22 columns — none found.
  • Confirmed squad changes vs. 2024-25: Leeds, Burnley and Sunderland (promoted) replace Ipswich, Leicester and Southampton (relegated).
  • Away-shots column is named AS in the raw file — a reserved SQL keyword, renamed on import to avoid query errors.

Statistical tests

Pearson correlation: clean sheets vs. league position r = −0.690, p = 0.0008 Significant
Pearson correlation: total cards vs. league position r = 0.291, p = 0.213 Not significant
Paired t-test: home goals vs. away goals t = 3.642, p = 0.0003 Significant

Interpretation: defensive solidity tracks final position closely and reliably; card counts do not, at conventional significance thresholds. Home advantage (1.53 vs. 1.22 goals/game) is a real, statistically robust effect this season, not a small-sample artifact.

Limitations

  • Single-season sample (380 matches) — team-level correlations especially should be read as descriptive of this season, not as a general law.
  • Correlation, not causation: clean sheets and final position are both downstream of overall team quality, not necessarily linked in a direct causal chain.
  • No expected-goals (xG), possession, or player-level data in this dataset — conclusions are limited to results-based and event-count statistics.
